Marine Predators Vs. Prey

Killer Whales

Killer Whales, known as Orcas, are large marine animals that are colored black and white. They live in the cold waters. They are extremely powerful, intelligent, quick, agressive, and creative. They eat fish and lots of other marine animals in the sea.

Seals

Seals are mammals that also live in the cold waters. They are colored in gray, silver, white, black, and brown. Though they always look sleak and wet, when they are dry they are covered in fluffy furr. They eat fish, shrill, and squid. Mostly just large amount of fish.

Seal and Orca Predator/Prey Relationship

A seal and an orca have one of the most fascinating — and tense — relationships in the ocean. Though both are marine mammals, their roles in the food chain couldn’t be more different. The orca, also known as the killer whale, is a top predator — powerful, intelligent, and highly social. Orcas often hunt in pods, using teamwork and strategy to catch their prey. One of their favorite foods? Seals. For the seal, this makes orcas both terrifying and awe-inspiring. Seals are quick, agile swimmers, and they rely on sharp instincts and the safety of ice floes or rocky shores to escape predators. When an orca approaches, seals must act fast — slipping into the water to hide beneath the ice, or scrambling onto land where the orca can’t reach. But there’s more to their relationship than just predator and prey. Scientists have observed how orcas’ hunting behaviors shape seal populations and influence where seals live and breed. In turn, seals play a role in maintaining the balance of fish and other marine life, which affects the orcas’ food sources, too.
