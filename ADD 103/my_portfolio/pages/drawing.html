Hallway-Line-Prespective Drawing

This hallway drawing was done in a random hallway in MCC. We learned about the vanishing point in class and had to make the drawing look like you are looking down a hallway.

Bones

In this project I had to create a bone, that I was physically looking at, and really had to highlight the lights in the bones. We used black prismacolore pencils and white. When doing this type of work you can never mix the two colors because that will make a gray color. Instead you can use the gray of the paper and/or closely line up the black and white colors together without letting them touch. This creates almost a 3D look!

Charcoal Drawing

For this piece we are still focusing on darks and lights. We spread the charcoal from the top of the paper to the very bottom and scrubbed to make a smooth background and cover the whole paper. After that was covered in an even black color, we used an eraser to find the objects from still-life and keep them the bright focus of the artwork. After finding all the lights, I went back in with the dark charcoal to get the shading right.

Still-Life Pencil Drawing

My professor was very creative with the still life in front of the classroom and in this piece we, simply with our pencil, and measuring the correct proportions in the objects, we made the shadows dark and kept the highlights the white of the paper.

Monster Drawing

I made a monster drawing the week of Halloween. I used the same techniques as I did with the bones artwork, using the white and black pencils. I found random, creepy objects from the classroom, and drew them how I saw them.

Ink Wash

Ink is very difficult to use. I had to mimic still-life still focusing on the lights and darks. I learned that starting very light first and then going in darker with the ink is the way to go.
